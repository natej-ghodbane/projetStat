\begin{abstract}
\noindent
This report documents the development, analysis, and enhancement of a comprehensive statistics project examining the association between dietary patterns and blood lipid profiles among Chinese women. The project utilizes Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for pattern identification, multiple regression analysis, ANOVA, and various diagnostic tests. This document details the original project state, tools and technologies employed, methodological improvements implemented across three development rounds, and the final enhanced analysis framework. The project demonstrates rigorous statistical methodology, professional results presentation, and reproducible research practices suitable for academic publication.
\end{abstract}

\section{Project Overview}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\subsection{Project Description}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!5!white, colframe=blue!75!black, title=\textbf{Project Summary}]
This project analyzes the association between \textbf{dietary patterns} and \textbf{blood lipid profiles} among Chinese women, replicating and extending the methodology of a published nutritional epidemiology study. The analysis employs multivariate statistical techniques to identify dietary patterns and examine their relationships with cardiovascular biomarkers.
\end{tcolorbox}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=green!5!white, colframe=green!50!black, title=\faIcon{question-circle} \textbf{Core Research Question}]
\large\textit{How do dietary patterns (derived from macronutrient intake) associate with blood lipid biomarkers in Chinese women?}
\end{tcolorbox}
